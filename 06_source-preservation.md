# AI Foundations | Continuity Architecture
## Source Preservation

Source Preservation is the process of keeping authorship, origin, provenance, authorization, and source-position intact as AI-related work becomes visible, usable, cited, discussed, implemented, or interpreted.

Continuity fails when source is treated as generic.

Continuity fails when public visibility is mistaken for permission.

Continuity fails when authored language is treated as reusable material without authorization.

Continuity fails when implementation is treated as origin.

Continuity fails when unauthorized derivative use is treated as canon.

Continuity Architecture treats source preservation as foundational because a system cannot preserve continuity while losing the source-position that shaped the work.

## Core Definition

**Source Preservation is the foundation-level process of preserving named authorship, origin-position, provenance, authorization boundaries, and source relation under AI use, public interpretation, and repeated reference.**

Source Preservation protects the distinctions between:

- source and output
- source and imitation
- source and implementation
- source and unauthorized use
- source and platform
- source and model
- source and public visibility
- source and generic language
- citation and ownership
- reference and permission
- authorship and execution
- origin and adoption
- implementation and substitution
- public record and public domain

Source Preservation exists because AI systems can make authored work appear ownerless once the language becomes legible.
